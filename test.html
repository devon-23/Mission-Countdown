Executive Summary

This document presents the complete engineering analysis for the interstellar vessel Hail Mary, constructed as humanity's response to the Astrophage crisis. The spacecraft is designed to transport a crewed mission 11.9 light-years to the Tau Ceti system while maintaining continuous life support, artificial gravity, propulsion, and thermal stability. All calculations herein utilize astrophage-derived propulsion and energy systems.

Conclusion

Analysis demonstrates that an astrophage-powered interstellar spacecraft is theoretically capable of reaching Tau Ceti within a single human lifetime. The design requires unprecedented energy harvesting, fuel production, and orbital construction infrastructure, but remains consistent with known astrophage behavior. Project Hail Mary therefore represents a physically achievable solution to the ongoing stellar luminosity crisis.
